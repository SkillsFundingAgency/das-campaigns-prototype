Choosing an apprentice

Apprenticeships are available at any level from GCSE to master’s degree, and they’re suitable for people who are at least 16, living in England and not in full-time education

If you’re interested in hiring an apprentice from outside your organisation, use Recruit an apprentice to advertise the vacancy and consider asking your training provider to help with the search

Apprenticeships are also ideal for upskilling your current staff - as long as the apprentice will develop new skills for their chosen occupation

Understanding the funding rules

You’ll get government funding to pay towards training and assessing your apprentice

The way this works depends on whether your organisation pays the apprenticeship levy (which is based on your annual pay bill - the amount you spend on things like wages, bonuses and commissions)

You’ll need to pay the apprentice’s salary, which will be at least the minimum wage rate for an apprentice

Training and assessing an apprentice

Your apprentices will receive apprenticeship training from you, their employer, alongside off-the-job training

You can work with your training provider to decide when, where and how off-the-job training is delivered

An end-point assessment is conducted by an independent organisation from the register of end-point assessment organisations, so apprentices are assessed consistently
