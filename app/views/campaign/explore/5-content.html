Section 01

First things first...

Before we discover your tailored tips, here are the key facts all employers should know about apprenticeships.

1

Your apprentices will receive off-the-job training from a training provider, teaching them the knowledge, skills and behaviours set out in the apprenticeship standard.

2

Apprenticeships are available at any level from GCSE to master’s degree.

3

You’ll need to supervise your apprentice on the job, training them to perform the work for which they’ve been employed.

4

They’re suitable for people who are at least 16, living in England and not in full-time education.

DEVELOPING FUTURE LEADERS

Apprenticeships form part of our talent pipeline, from which we identify future leaders. In our experience, apprentices are hungry to learn and develop new skills. They bring a fresh perspective, commitment to our values, and high rates of retention. They help us to develop our workforce with the skills that we need as a business, now and in the future.

WORKING WITH THE RIGHT TRAINING PROVIDER

Our apprentices’ experience both on and off the job is fundamental to the success of the programme and their future development. We work closely with our training providers in supporting our apprentices to gain a breadth of experience that’s relevant to their study.

LEVELING THE PLAYING FIELD

In 2015 we removed academic entry criteria from our student recruitment process, broadening our pool of talent. We also operate a ‘blind CV’ policy that helps us to create a level playing field for all candidates and promotes greater diversity among our workforce.

Section 05

Funding your apprenticeship

Read more about funding your apprenticeship.

  1. As an employer with a pay bill of less than £3 million per year, you’ll get funding from the government to pay towards training and assessing apprentices.
  2. The amount of funding you get depends on the number of employees you have and the apprentice you’re hiring.
  3. You’ll need to pay the apprentice’s salary, which will be at least the apprentice minimum wage rate (currently £4.15).
  1. As an employer with a pay bill of more than £3 million, you’re required to pay the apprenticeship levy.
  2. You’ll receive funds through the apprenticeship service to spend on training and assessing your apprentices.
  3. You’ll need to pay the apprentice’s salary, which will be at least the apprentice minimum wage rate (currently £4.15).
  1. If your pay bill is less than £3 million per year, you’ll get funding from the government to pay towards training and assessing apprentices. The amount depends on the number of employees you have and the apprentice you’re hiring.
  2. If your pay bill is more than £3 million, you’re required to pay the apprenticeship levy. This means you’ll receive funds through the apprenticeship service to spend on training and assessing your apprentices.
  3. Either way, you’ll need to pay the apprentice’s salary. This will be at least the apprentice minimum wage rate (currently £4.15).
