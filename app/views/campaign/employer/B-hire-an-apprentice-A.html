Who can be an apprentice?

Apprenticeships help you to bring valuable new skills into your business by training a current employee or hiring someone new.

They’re suitable for people at any level, as long as they:

  • will start their apprenticeship after the last Friday in June of the academic year in which they have their 16th birthday
  • can complete the apprenticeship within the time they have available
  • won’t be asked to contribute financially to the cost of training or end-point assessment
  • will spend at least 50% of their working hours in England over the durationof the apprenticeship
  • have the right to work in England, and eligible residency

Preparing to find an apprentice

If you’ve decided to hire an apprentice from outside your organisation, a person specification is a good place to start. This should include:

  • essential and desirable knowledge criteria
  • previous experience they should have
  • the specific skills you’re looking for in the successful candidate

You should also write a job description, including:

  • a job title
  • the main duties and purpose of the role
  • information about the company and the job location
  • the salary you’ll pay the apprentice

By hiring an apprentice, you’ll make a valuable investment in your business with the government’s support. Before you start your search, you should consider the long-term impact the apprentice could make within your organisation – for example, think about their future career prospects and salary.

Advertising your apprenticeship vacancy

Recruit an apprentice is the official government service for posting and managing apprenticeship vacancies and traineeship opportunities in England. The system can be managed by training providers on your behalf or by you directly.

To create your advert, complete the apprenticeship vacancy template. Write as clearly as possible so candidates who are searching through the vacancies can quickly understand whether your apprenticeship is right for them. You’ll need to include:

  • the information from your person specification and job description
  • a closing date for the vacancy
  • potential interview and start dates

Budding apprentices can search and apply for apprenticeship vacancies using Find an apprenticeship.

Other ways to find an apprentice include:

  • holding open days at your organisation
  • participating in shows, such as WorldSkills UK Live
  • inviting potential apprentices to your organisation for a day

Choosing the right apprentice

You can work with your training provider to choose the best method of shortlisting candidates who have applied for your vacancy. You might ask them tosend you copies of all the applications that have been submitted, or to perform an initial sift on your behalf and forward you any suitable applications to save you time.

To select candidates to interview, you should refer to the job description and person specification, eliminating those who don’t have the basic requirements for the job.

Remember, when a candidate attends an interview, it’s their chance to form an impression of your company as much as it is for you to find out about them. First impressions count!
