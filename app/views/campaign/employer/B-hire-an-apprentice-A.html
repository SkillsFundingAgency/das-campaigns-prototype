Who can be an apprentice?

Apprenticeships are great for hiring someone new, as well as upskilling a current employee.

They’re available at various levels, so they could be suitable for anyone. For example, an intermediate apprenticeship (level 2) is equivalent to GCSE level, and a degree apprenticeship (level 6 or 7) is equivalent to a bachelor’s or master’s degree.

Entry requirements

Apprentices must live in England, and they must not already be in full-time education or taking another apprenticeship.

They must be 16 or over by the end of the summer holidays. If your apprentice turns 16 during the current academic year, the apprenticeship must start after the last Friday in June.

Upskilling your current staff

Apprenticeships are suitable for bringing in new staff, but they’re also ideal for upskilling or retraining your existing workforce.

Preparing to find an apprentice

If you’ve decided to hire an apprentice from outside your business, you’ll need to think about what you’re looking for. A person specification is a good place to start, including:

  • essential and desirable knowledge they’ll have
  • previous experience they should have
  • the specific skills you’re looking for

You should also write a job description, including:

  • a job title
  • the main duties and purpose of the role
  • information about the company and the job location
  • the salary you’ll pay the apprentice

By employing an apprentice, you’ll make a valuable investment in your business with the government’s support. Think carefully about the long-term impact your apprentice could make, and consider their future career prospects and salary.

Advertising apprenticeships

Next, it’s time to think about where to advertise the apprenticeship.

Recruit an apprentice is the official government service for posting and managing apprenticeship adverts and traineeship opportunities in England. You can manage this yourself, or training providers can recruit on your behalf.

To create your advert, complete the template. Write as clearly as possible, so candidates searching through the vacancies can quickly understand whether your apprenticeship is right for them.

Budding apprentices can search and apply for apprenticeship vacancies using Find an apprenticeship service.

Choosing the right apprentice

You can work with your training provider to choose the best method of shortlisting candidates who have applied for your apprenticeship. You might ask them to choose the most suitable applications on your behalf.

Think about how you’ll interview your candidates - for example, you could hold remote interviews using digital platforms.

Interview questions to ask apprentices

Take time to think about what you really need to know and how you'll find out if the candidate is right for your business.

Here are some sample questions:

  • can you tell me an interesting fact about yourself?
  • what would you consider your biggest achievement in school/college/work?
  • what's your timekeeping like?
  • do you work well in a team?
  • what are your strengths?
  • what are your weaknesses?
  • why do you think we should hire you?
  • why would you like to work for our organisation?
  • do you have any questions you'd like to ask us?
