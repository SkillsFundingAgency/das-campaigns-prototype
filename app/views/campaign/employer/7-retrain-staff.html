Develop new skills within your existing workforce

Apprenticeships aren’t just suitable for bringing in new staff – they’re also ideal for upskilling or retraining your current workforce. Perhaps you have an employee who’s keen to develop their existing skills, or someone who has showna flair for something completely new. Giving them the opportunity to complete an apprenticeship allows you to fill key skill gaps in your business, while boostingmotivation and retention of your staff.

Any age, any level – but the learning must be new

Apprenticeships are suitable for people of any age, no matter what level they’re at. The important thing is that the apprentice will develop new skills to help themsucceed in their chosen occupation. The apprenticeship must be linked to their job, and the learning must be different to any qualifications they already hold. If they’re taking a repeat or lower-level apprenticeship, you must provide evidence that it’s supporting the apprentice to acquire new skills and knowledge. They must gain significant new learning from the apprenticeship.

You don’t have to give them a diferent job

The employee doesn’t necessarily need to be given a different job to complete the apprenticeship, and you’re not required to change any of the terms or conditions of their employment. The apprentice must be genuinely learning new skills to the benefit of the wider economy, and you’ll be required to create and sign an apprenticeship agreement. You’ll continue to pay them a salary – at least the National Minimum Wage relevant to their age.
