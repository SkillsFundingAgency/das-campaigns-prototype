Apprenticeship funding from the government

Apprenticeship training and assessment

As an employer with a pay bill of more than £3 million, you’re required to pay the apprenticeship levy. You’ll receive funds through the apprenticeship service to spend on training and assessing your apprentices.

The amount of funding entering your account each month is calculated as follows:

  • the levy you declare to HMRC through the PAYE process
  • multiplied by the proportion of your bill paid to your workforce who live in England
  • plus a 10% government top-up on this amount

When you’re ready to get started, you’ll need to sign in or create a new account on the apprenticeship service.

Apprenticeship funding from the government

Apprenticeship training and assessment

As an employer with a pay bill of less than £3 million per year, you’ll share apprenticeship costs with the government if you have at least 50 employees. This means:

  • you’ll pay 5% towards the cost of training and assessing your apprentice
  • the government will pay the remaining 95%, up to the funding band maximum
  • if your apprentice’s training and assessment cost more than this, you’ll pay the difference

Employers with fewer than 50 employees

If you’re an employer with fewer than 50 employees, the government will fund all the apprenticeship training costs up to the funding band maximum if the apprentice is:

  • between 16 and 18 years old (or 15 years old if the apprentice’s 16th birthday is between the last Friday of June and 31 August)
  • between 19 and 24 years old and has either an Education, Health and Care (EHC) plan provided by their local authority or has been in the care oftheir local authority

This means you won’t have to pay the 5% co-investment amount.

Training young apprentices

All employers will receive £1,000 if, at the start of the apprenticeship, the apprentice is:

  • between 16 and 18 years old (or 15 years old if the apprentice’s 16th birthday is between the last Friday of June and 31 August)
  • between 19 and 24 years old and has either an Education, Health and Care (EHC) plan provided by their local authority or has been in the care oftheir local authority

Apprenticeship costs paid by you

Your apprentice’s salary

You must pay your apprentice at least the National Minimum Wage for their normal working hours and the training they undertake as part of the apprenticeship.

If they’re under 19 or aged 19 or over and in their first year, they’re entitled to the apprentice rate. If they’re aged 19 or over and they’ve completed their first year, they must be paid at least the minimum wage rate for their age.

Other costs

An apprenticeship is a long-term investment from you and the government to help grow your business, so it’s worth committing as much time and effort as you can to maximise its impact. You’ll need to:

  • pay any administrative costs to bring your apprentice on board
  • supervise the apprentice throughout their apprenticeship
  • provide on-the-job training to help them develop new skills

Depending on your circumstances, you may choose to:

  • assign a member of staff to be your apprentice’s mentor
  • cover or contribute towards the apprentice’s travel costs
  • arrange weekly catch-ups to check how your apprentice is progressing

Transferring funds to another employer

If you have unused apprenticeship funds, you can transfer them to another employer using the apprenticeship service.

You can find employers who want to receive a transfer by:

  • working with employers in your supply chain
  • getting in touch with employers in your industry
  • contacting an Apprenticeship Training Agency (ATA)
  • working with regional partners

Paying for apprenticeships using a transfer of apprenticeship funds

Employers who already have an apprenticeship service account can transfer unused apprenticeship funds to another employer.

Transferred funds can be used to pay for new apprenticeship training and assessment for apprenticeship standards. They can’t be used for apprenticeship frameworks, which are being phased out.
