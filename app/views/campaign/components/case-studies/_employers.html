NORFOLK & NORWICH UNIVERSITY HOSPITAL NHS FOUNDATION TRUST

OUR WORKFORCE OF THE FUTURE

Apprentices are our workforce of the future, and we’ve invested in growing our own trained staff. It benefits us to have apprentices in our business – not least because 92% of them choose to stay within the organisation after completing their apprenticeships.

A GOOD TRAINING PROVIDER IS KEY

We match each job description with an apprenticeship standard or framework, and work collaboratively with training providers to deliver them. We have strong working relationships with our providers, so they understand our needs as a business and how we support our apprentices.

FINDING THE RIGHT APPRENTICE

We advertise our vacancies on NHS Jobs and the Trust website, and we recruit to our PRIDE values (People focussed, Respect, Integrity, Dedication and Excellence). A member of our apprenticeship team always interviews with the recruiting manager to ensure candidates are aware of the requirements of our apprenticeships programme.

TROUP BYWATERS + ANDERS

HOMEGROWN TALENT TO BOOST OUR BUSINESS

At Troup Bywaters + Anders, we’re addressing the skills gap in our industry by developing engineers of the future. As our homegrown talent matures, apprentices are contributing to our financial growth and size. We recruit all year round to attract the right people – from those due to complete qualifications to NEET (Not in Education, Employment or Training) talent.

NUMBERS THAT SPEAK FOR THEMSELVES

Since embedding our apprenticeship programme, we’ve seen tangible business benefits. Our retention rate for apprentices is 85%, our apprentice intake has contributed to a 100% reduction in agency fees at technician level, and we’ve opened a Glasgow office to meet our growth objective.

CREATING OPPORTUNITIES FOR EVERYONE

We form close relationships with our training providers, communicating and collaborating to develop courses tailored to our business. We’ve also acted as ‘trailblazers’ to maintain a professional route into the industry that’s available forall, no matter their background.

We’re addressing the skills gap in our industry by developing engineers of the future.

DEVELOPING FUTURE LEADERS

Apprenticeships form part of our talent pipeline, from which we identify future leaders. In our experience, apprentices are hungry to learn and develop new skills. They bring a fresh perspective, commitment to our values, and high rates of retention. They help us to develop our workforce with the skills that we need as a business, now and in the future.

WORKING WITH THE RIGHT TRAINING PROVIDER

LEVELING THE PLAYING FIELD

In 2015 we removed academic entry criteria from our student recruitment process, broadening our pool of talent. We also operate a ‘blind CV’ policy that helps us to create a level playing field for all candidates and promotes greater diversity among our workforce.

New recruits bring fresh ideas and a talent pipeline, while increasing the diversity of our workforce – but apprenticeships are also great for upskilling existing staff. BT

DISCOVERING THE BENEFITS OF APPRENTICESHIPS

BT Group has been offering apprenticeships for over 60 years. We have statisticalevidence that apprentices stay with us, are loyal, get promoted, support other apprentices, and are very engaged in our business. We’ve been able to create lots of new programmes to develop skills our staff need for the future.

TEAMING UP WITH TRAINING PROVIDERS

BT Group is an Employer Provider and, where we have the expertise, we deliver apprenticeships ourselves. When we outsource delivery, we look for providers who can offer quality programmes across a wide geography with a range of delivery options. We have Service Level Agreements with our providers to ensurethat we maintain a close relationship.

CREATING A DIVERSE WORKFORCE

We advertise in the media, through the National Apprenticeship Service and through other sites. We have moved to strengths-based recruitment, which is helping us to recruit more successful applicants from diverse backgrounds. Since making this change, we’ve seen improved success rates and evidence of equalityand parity across gender and ethnicity groups.

FIRST BUS

A WELL-ROUNDED EXPERIENCE

We currently run four engineering apprenticeships, providing apprentices with skills, knowledge and technical expertise by allowing them to work across the entire First Bus fleet – including inspection, servicing, fault diagnosis and repair. We’re continually looking for innovative ways to cross-skill apprentices, so they’re fully capable to deal with all aspects of bus engineering.

COLLABORATING WITH TRAINING PROVIDERS

The relationship with our training provider is key to the apprentice’s journey. We work closely with providers to monitor our apprentices’ progress. We review providers’ contracts on a quarterly basis, and run focus groups and online surveys for apprentices to provide feedback on their experiences.

FINDING THE RIGHT APPRENTICE

To find an apprentice, we use online assessment tools and hold practical assessment centres locally. Managers and mentors are involved in the selection of our apprentices, as they will be the ones that support the apprentice throughout their journey. To manage the process, we use the National Apprenticeship Service in partnership with our providers.

RSPCA

INCREASING OUR TALENT POOL

There are loads of benefits to hiring apprentices. Apprenticeships help us to develop knowledge and skills within the organisation, improve employee motivation, provide development opportunities, feed into succession planning – and of course, increase our talent pool!

GETTING STARTED

Over the past two years, we’ve conducted a great deal of research, using Find apprenticeship training and the Institute forApprenticeship website. To get started, we engage with training providers and meet with potential apprentices and line managers to ensure a shared understanding, making sure the training isright for the individual, role and organisation.

FINDING AN APPRENTICE

To find an apprentice, we recruit internally, put out communications, engage withline managers for recommendations and refer to external sources. We advertise with the training provider, using Recruit an apprentice on the government website, and Vacancy Snapshot on the Amazing Apprenticeships website.

Apprenticeships help us to develop knowledge and skills within the organisation – and of course, increase our talent pool!

Laura Swan Apprenticeships Development Manager
