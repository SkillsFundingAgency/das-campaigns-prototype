Setting off on a career path can be overwhelming for young people. As a parent, you’ll play a key role in guiding and supporting them as they take their next step. Whether they don’t thrive in an academic environment or they get top grades, apprenticeships shouldn’t be overlooked. In fact, with new higher and degree apprenticeships popping up each year, they could get the best of both worlds – learning while earning at the same time.

Apprenticeships in a nutshell

Apprenticeships have evolved significantly in recent years. They offer the chance to earn a wage, while learning important skills and gaining qualifications. An apprentice spends at least 20% of their working hours completing off-the-job training, developing transferable skills for a wide range of career paths. They could even get a degree as part of the apprenticeship. Alongside their training, they gain hands-on experience in a real-world setting – an invaluable asset when it comes to future employment.

They’ll earn a salary – and their training will be paid for too

An apprenticeship can boost a young person’s career, while offering great financial benefits. They’ll be paid (at least the national minimum wage, but often a more competitive salary), and they’ll spend at least 20% of their time completing training – at no cost to them. This could include working towards a degree or other qualifications. At the same time, they’ll gain experience in a real workplace, and develop important soft skills as they transition into the world of work.

They’ll gain experience in a relevant role, plus career-boosting skills

From digital marketing and teaching to dental healthcare and textile manufacturing, there’s a standard to suit everyone – and the number of apprenticeships available is constantly on the rise. You can use the Find an apprenticeship tool to see what’s on offer. Once they’ve started their apprenticeship, they’ll gain important insight into their chosen industry, while learning about a host of roles within the organisation. If they decide the occupation they’ve chosen isn’t for them, they’ll be equipped with skills and qualifications to make them extremely employable, no matter what step they take next. In fact, 88% of apprentices remain in employment after completing their apprenticeship.

Apprenticeships have the following equivalent educational levels:

Name Level Equivalent educational level
Intermediate 2 GCSE
Advanced 3 A level
Higher 4,5,6 and 7 Foundation degree and above
Degree 6 and 7 Bachelor’s or master’s degree

Some apprenticeships may also give you an additional qualification, such as a diploma.

Next steps

If they’re not sure which route to take, it’s worth remembering that they don’t have to decide straight away. They can choose to apply for both university degrees and apprenticeships, for example, and make their final decision when they’ve had more time to research and reflect.

In the meantime, there are lots of other useful resources out there to help you support them.

  • Head over to the Amazing Apprentices website to discover a host of resources, including packs specially put together for parents
  • The National Careers Service website is packed with tips, including interview advice and the top 10 questions they could be asked
  • Which? University has teamed up with the National Apprenticeship Service to create a downloadable guide to higher and degree apprenticeships
  • Check out the government website for more information
